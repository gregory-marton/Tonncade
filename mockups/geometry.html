Tonnetz Tetris — the one decision to make first

Same Tonnetz lattice in both panels. The question is just: what does one Tetris cell represent? Click around to hear it, then hit “Play the sample piece.” Sound starts on your first click (browser rule).

Option A

A cell is a note (a node)

Closest to traditional Tetris: one block = one note. A 4-cell piece sounds 4 notes together — a tetrachord / cluster. The highlighted piece is a “T”: three fifths in a row plus a third.

click a note…

Tip: notes rise in pitch as you move up/right — this is the real “Harmonic Table” layout.

Option B

A cell is a chord (a triangle)

Most uniquely Tonnetz: every little triangle is a major or minor triad, and neighbors share notes. A 4-cell piece is 4 chords that flow into each other — a tiny progression. The highlighted piece is a 4-triangle strip; you’ll hear it strummed chord-by-chord.

click a triangle…

Up-triangles are major, down-triangles are minor. Three triangles meeting at a node = the three triads containing that note.

Once you’ve heard both: which feels like the Tetris you want to play — placing notes (A) or placing chords (B)? Tell me in the terminal. (We can also mix later; this is just about the core grid.)
